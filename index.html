About Siem Reap

Siem Reap is a province in northwestern Cambodia, famous for the Angkor Archaeological Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The province is home to the majestic temples of Angkor, including the iconic Angkor Wat, the largest religious monument in the world. Siem Reap also boasts vibrant markets, bustling nightlife, and rich cultural traditions. Whether you are exploring ancient temples, enjoying local cuisine, or immersing yourself in local traditions, Siem Reap offers an unforgettable experience for every visitor.

The Angkor Archaeological Park

The Angkor Archaeological Park is one of Southeast Asia's most important historical sites. Home to over 1,000 temples, it spans 162.6 hectares and attracts millions of tourists each year.
